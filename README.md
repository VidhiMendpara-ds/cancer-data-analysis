Analyzed 50,000 global cancer patient records using Python (Pandas, Seaborn) to identify demographic patterns and risk factor distributions, providing insights for healthcare strategy and resource planning.

# Global Cancer Patients Analysis (2015-2024)

## Project Overview
Comprehensive analysis of 50,000 cancer patient records to identify patterns in demographics, risk factors, treatment outcomes, and survival rates across different regions and cancer types.

## Business Problem
Understanding patient demographics, risk factor distributions, and treatment patterns to support healthcare decision-making and resource allocation.

## Dataset
- **Source:** Global cancer patient records (2015-2024)
- **Records:** 50,000 patients
- **Features:** 15 columns including demographics, risk factors, cancer types, treatment costs, and survival metrics

## Key Features Analyzed
- Patient demographics (Age, Gender, Country)
- Risk factors (Genetic, Environmental, Lifestyle)
- Cancer types and staging
- Treatment costs and survival outcomes
- Severity scoring

## Technical Skills Demonstrated
- **Data Analysis:** Pandas, NumPy
- **Data Visualization:** Seaborn, Matplotlib
- **Data Cleaning:** Missing value handling, duplicate detection
- **Statistical Analysis:** Descriptive statistics, distribution analysis
- **Tools:** Jupyter Notebook, Python

## Key Findings
- **Age Distribution:** Broad patient age range (20-89 years) with mean age of 54.4 years
- **Gender Distribution:** Balanced representation across Male (33.6%), Female (33.4%), and Other (33.0%) categories
- **Data Quality:** Zero duplicate records with complete data across all 50,000 entries
- **Global Scope:** Patients from multiple countries including UK, China, Pakistan, Brazil, USA
